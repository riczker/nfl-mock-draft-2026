With Love gone, the Titans go best player available in Reese. Potentially with the highest ceiling in the draft, Reese is projected to play edge but can also play off the ball and cover effectively. NFL Comp · Garrett Wilson

Pick
Miami Dolphins
Francis Mauigoa
OT Miami (FL)

Watching Mauigoa in the CFB Playoffs and his tape at Miami is something everyone should consider doing at some point. One of the videos was titled "The Samoan Steamroller" — which I really hope sticks. Point is, he makes everything look so easy. There are some concerns with his back, and once again I'm not a doctor or NFL executive, but I consider pick 11 about the latest he'll be selected. If these didn't exist, he'd be the unanimous top OL in the draft.

NFL Comp · Quinyon Mitchell
13
Pick
Los Angeles Rams
Makai Lemon
WR USC

For a team that doesn't exactly have a glaring need, they draft the best player available here. Lemon is a football player first. Some receivers get moved to corner in college because they're not good enough — Lemon was so good at both in high school that there was a legitimate debate on where he'd play. He chose wisely, winning the Biletnikoff Award as the best WR in college in 2025. His hands are beyond elite, and his RAC ability is his biggest strength — a true human joystick. That RAC ability will certainly be maximized in a Sean McVay offense. As a slightly smaller guy he struggles against press coverage, but should enter the league as a great slot receiver right away. The main takeaway: once he gets the ball in his hands, good luck.

NFL Comp · Amon-Ra St. Brown
14
Pick
Trade
Carolina Panthers · via trade
Kenyon Sadiq
TE Oregon

Bryce Young and the Panthers offense really took a leap after the addition of offensive rookie of the year Tetairoa McMillan in the 2025 draft, and they continue to bolster their offense by trading up one pick in front of division rival Tampa Bay to add Kenyon Sadiq. I hate the phrase "tight end with wide receiver skills," but I'm gonna use it here because that's what Sadiq is. He's got a RAS of 9.49 and is in the 99th percentile for his jumping skills and 40 time for tight ends. As a blocker, he's not amazing — but what do you expect for a 6'2" TE? Is he the worst blocker I've ever seen? No. Is he the best? Also no, but the effort is always there and it's something coaches can solve with technique improvements. The biggest flaw in his game seems to be his drop rate, which was absurdly high this year. It could very well be a fluke since Sadiq is known for his hands and acrobatic catches, but his job is to catch the ball and not drop it (thanks, Captain Obvious). Bonus: he turned 21 in March and is one of the youngest prospects in the draft.

NFL Comp · Evan Engram (but much better)
15
Pick
Tampa Bay Buccaneers
Akheem Mesidor
EDGE Miami (FL)

If production was the only thing NFL scouts cared about, Mesidor would be selected much higher. However, he just turned 25 and projects to have a lower ceiling than other premier pass rushers. At Miami, him and Rueben Bain put up extremely similar numbers — he ranked just behind Bain and Bailey in pass rush win rate (21.3%), and typically anyone over 20% is a surefire first rounder. He has an injury history to take note of, but was healthy last year and highly productive. He's a beast: super technically savvy with a relentless motor, and has shown the ability to shift inside. He lost some weight before his career-best 2025 season — we'll see how teams elect to use him, especially Todd Bowles, who runs a wacky 3-4 defense with crazy blitz packages and consistently drops edges into coverage. Most mocks have him as a Day 2 pick because of the age, but I think he's too good to ignore — and a team like Tampa that wants to win now will love him. If his medicals check out, he projects as a Day 1 starter that will positively impact a Bucs defense that was pretty subpar last season (5.7 yards/play allowed).

New York Jets · via trade (Sauce Gardner)
Jermod McCoy
CB Tennessee

McCoy has had a lot of injury concerns — sitting out last season with a torn ACL — but his 2024 tape was insanely good, objectively better than Delane's tape this season. For that reason, he's my CB2 — but don't be shocked if he ends up the top corner off the board. McCoy answered the critics at his pro day in March (40 time: 4.38, Vert: 38", Broad: 10'7"). Seems like he'll be okay. The Jets traded Sauce Gardner to the Colts for two firsts, and will use one of them on his replacement. McCoy is long, physical, and should be a stud if healthy.

Detroit Lions
Monroe Freeling
OT Georgia

A guy on Reddit said it best: "If you take Freeling, you may get Kolton Miller and be set at left tackle for a decade, or he could flame out like the dozens and dozens of athletic R1 tackles that get drafted for their athletic traits and potential. It really depends on how confident the team is in their ability to develop O-line talent." It's also worth mentioning that he primarily plays left tackle, which would let Penei Sewell stick around on the right side where he's been so dominant.

NFL Comp · Kolton Miller / Spencer Brown
18
Pick
Minnesota Vikings
Dillon Thieneman
S Oregon

In mock drafts, this pick shows up almost as consistently as Mendoza to the Raiders — and for good reason. The Vikings secondary is pretty bad, ranking in the bottom 5 according to PFF. The secondary relies heavily on Brian Flores' defensive scheme to generate pressure, masking their deficiencies in coverage. The main reason it's so likely for the Vikings to take Thieneman is the inevitable release of Harrison Smith, a projected post-June 1 cut. I hate the argument of positional value here, because I really believe Thieneman will be one of the 30 best players in this class. He's an athletic freak (9.72 RAS, 4.36 40 time), super physical, and has a tremendous IQ — always seeming to be in the right spot on tape. Still needs to improve as a tackler at the next level, and isn't the biggest dude in the world, but plays bigger than his size. He'd fit perfectly in this scheme and fills a huge hole in the Vikings' back end.

EDGE Auburn

Faulk is a freak athlete. The Chiefs love taking these raw, high-upside developmental guys, and it typically pays dividends. NFL Comp · Arik Armstead
21
Pick
Pittsburgh Steelers
Caleb Lomu
OT Utah

Lomu is super athletic and has potential to be awesome. He's got great lateral quickness and mirroring ability in pass protection, and his tape suggests he's a smooth mover in space — making him ideal for zone-blocking schemes that new HC Mike McCarthy wants to implement. Lomu's got strong hands and natural recovery balance if initially beaten. His measurables aren't great — short arms and not the mass you want in an NFL tackle — yet he allowed zero sacks in 383 pass-blocking snaps during the 2025 season in a good Big 12 conference. McCarthy has been showing surprising confidence in the Steelers OL, but that won't stop him from drafting one. In 11 of McCarthy's 18 drafts, his team's first selection was either an offensive or defensive lineman — including his last four drafts in Dallas.

NFL Comp · What André Dillard could've been
22
Pick
Los Angeles Chargers
Peter Woods
DT Clemson

Woods is an athletic freak. His first-step explosiveness and overall athletic profile for a 300+ lb defender is elite. Woods has insane upper-body power, really violent hands, and the agility to occasionally line up as a jumbo edge rusher. He ran a 4.86 40 time, and that definitely shows up on tape — he somehow manages to run by guys on the inside and consistently chases ball carriers downfield. Like many 2026 prospects, the main weaknesses come with arm size, and he's also notorious for his lack of gap discipline, occasionally taking unnecessary risks rather than holding his run fit. This can be resolved with good coaching, and Jim Harbaugh is exactly that. The Chargers could use some more help up the middle, so it would be awesome to see them take a chance on a super high-upside prospect in Woods.

NFL Comp · Christian Wilkins
23
Pick
Philadelphia Eagles
Omar Cooper Jr.
WR Indiana

Assuming the AJ Brown trade to the Patriots is happening, I really like the fit of Omar Cooper on the Eagles. Already used to playing in a run-heavy system at IU, Cooper is a great and willing blocker — the IU staff used him like Puka Nacua as a lead blocker in the B gap against guys way bigger than him. Cooper was a three-sport athlete in college (football, basketball, track), so it goes without saying that his measurables are good. His YAC ability is great, but he's a little raw in terms of route running and separation against man coverage. Facing zone is a different story — his feel for finding pockets in coverage is elite, probably his biggest strength. In fact, PFF had him as the second-best WR against zone in all of CFB, behind only the great Jeremiah Smith. Cooper isn't an A+ prospect, but it's clear he's a winner — plays inside and outside, can play within a system, and predominantly makes the right play. He'd be a great fit in Philly's offense.

NFL Comp · Ladd McConkey
24
Pick
Cleveland Browns
Denzel Boston
WR Washington

Boston is a literal ball vacuum. His catch radius is insane, and he LOVES to go up and get a 50/50 ball — his tape is really fun to watch. His main concerns center around his speed and ability to create separation against NFL cornerbacks. Historically, his YAC ability is low compared to other top prospects, and his bread and butter is contested catches (which he's awesome at). His route tree is also a little limited, likely a product of the system he was playing in at Washington. Regardless, Cleveland has a desperate need for a WR — specifically an X — and Boston can fill that beautifully. I really hope he can develop and improve his weaknesses, because I think he can be really, really good.

Parker has elite run-stopping discipline and really strong, violent hands that annoy blockers. NFL Comp · Trey Hendrickson
26
Pick
Buffalo Bills
KC Concepcion
WR Texas A&M

Joe Brady loves these small, twitchy receivers. KC has elite lateral quickness and separation ability — he's a YAC machine with a complete route tree, often used in motions, screens, and jet sweeps. He also offers high-level special teams value as a punt returner. Plus, he was born in Rochester, NY and the Bills are his dad's favorite team. Would be pretty cool! I don't think he can be a consistent WR1 because of his smaller frame, lack of elite speed, and high drop rate. There will be corners who can mirror him and hamper his ability to move the chains. I love his YAC ability, but Buffalo already has so many guys with that trait — DJ Moore, Shakir, Kincaid, etc. One underrated ability: KC led Texas A&M with 8 red-zone touchdowns and was the SEC leader in receiving TDs with 9. The Bills can sometimes struggle to pass in the red area, so KC should help. Assuming nothing crazy happens, I do believe Buffalo goes WR with this pick — and KC is a perfect scheme fit who can hopefully improve their red-zone offense.

NFL Comp · Wan'Dale Robinson
27
Pick
San Francisco 49ers
Kadyn Proctor
OT Alabama

The 6'7", 352-pound giant from Bama is one of my favorite players in the draft. The image of him catching a swing pass and almost scoring against Georgia lives rent-free in my head. Proctor has elite raw power and massive anchor strength that stonewalls pass rushers. He has pretty nimble feet for his size and excels as a "road-grader" in gap-blocking schemes. Being so big comes with its issues — Proctor struggles when edge rushers are aligned widely, which may indicate he'd be better suited as a guard, similar to Mekhi Becton's career arc. Apparently the Niners see Proctor as a "special player" who could fill two critical needs on a win-now timeline: the heir to Trent Williams (entering his age-38 season) and a potential immediate starter at guard. This marriage with Kyle Shanahan introduces some really exciting possibilities.

NFL Comp · Mekhi Becton / Trent Brown
28
Pick
Houston Texans
Kayden McDonald
DT Ohio State

Big Mac had a heck of a 2025 campaign, winning Big Ten Defensive Lineman of the Year and being named a unanimous All-American. A big reason was his elite run-stopping ability — he led the FBS in 2025 with 30 run stuffs. He's got literal tree-trunk lower-body power that makes him nearly immovable against double teams, and was a huge reason the OSU defense was so elite these past few years. Like every other prospect in this draft, he's got "short" arms — at this point there's gotta be something in the water. His tape suggests he's more of a pocket collapser than a refined pass rusher, but honestly that's a perfect fit on a Texans team that invests nearly $100M annually on their two star edge rushers. If he can refine his pass-rushing ability, he could be an All-Pro — but that's a massive IF. For now, he's a high-floor run stopper that will add a lot to an already elite Texans defense. Could easily be DT1 in this class.

NFL Comp · DJ Reader / Derrick Brown

Pick

Dallas Cowboys · via trade
Malachi Lawrence
EDGE UCF

With their second of two first-round picks, the Cowboys select Malachi Lawrence. It's unlike Jerry to not draft an offensive lineman named Tyler in Round 1, but there's no one good! It also helps that this guy is a freak of nature — though quite a raw prospect. Since the combine, he's flown up draft boards. I saw a Reddit post saying that in under a week, Lawrence went from +400 to -140 on sportsbooks to be a first-round pick. That type of movement doesn't just happen for no reason. He posted a 9.90 RAS, ran a 4.52 40 time at 6'4", 250 lbs, has 35" arms, plays extremely physical, and has great bend — and just earned All-Big 12 First Team honors. The biggest weaknesses are common with raw prospects: inconsistent snap timing, a tendency to play too upright, and occasionally biting on play-fakes. This can all be corrected with coaching. Lucky for Dallas, Lawrence is described as a relentless worker and a high-IQ player who switched to defense late in high school. He is praised for his insane motor and unselfish play on a pretty bad UCF team. The upside is certainly there.

NFL Comp · Nik Bonitto
30
Pick
Miami Dolphins
Colton Hood
CB Tennessee

The Dolphins are more desperate for a corner than a camel is for water. Hood has elite physicality and shows a lot of toughness in run support — a seriously underrated aspect of his game. He excels in press-man coverage, using a disruptive punch to throw off receiver timing. He is very "ball aware" with high-level ball skills, leading to a lot of picks and PBUs. His tape suggests he's a super aggressive press-man bully that can potentially turn into the type of corner you can confidently leave on an island. That aggressive nature led to a fair bit of penalties at Tennessee and will surely continue in the NFL. He occasionally overplays routes, leaving him vulnerable to double moves — but this is prevalent with a lot of young, aggressive corners. The good news is that a few times when he did get beat, he recovered really quickly thanks to his insane speed. Hood clocked a 4.44s 40-yard dash with a 40.5-inch vertical at the combine — and reportedly ran closer to 4.35–4.41 at his pro day. He's fast. He's regarded as a high-character competitor who stepped into a CB1 role at Tennessee following Jermod McCoy's injury and was praised by coaches for his great work ethic and quick adjustment to the spotlight.

NFL Comp · Terrion Arnold
31
Pick
New England Patriots
Kamari Ramsey
S USC

For the second time in 40 years, three safeties will be drafted in Round 1. While some may consider this a slight reach, Ramsey is someone who has really stuck out to me on tape and has shot up my rankings recently. Touted as a coach on the field and a respected leader, Ramsey was named a captain at USC shortly after transferring from UCLA — and wore the green dot in spring practices to handle defensive communications, which is really uncommon for a safety. Ramsey was consistently listed as a prospect the Pats showed interest in, with scouting reports noting him as a fit for their system due to his versatility, football IQ, and experience in the slot. The scheme fit makes a ton of sense, and he can contribute effectively at safety, nickel, or on special teams.

NFL Comp · Xavier McKinney

Trade
Arizona Cardinals · via trade
Ty Simpson
QB Alabama

There are so many QB-needy teams lurking at the beginning of Round 2, and Seahawks GM John Schneider has made it clear they'll "be looking to move back" in the draft — so it's inevitable this pick will not belong to Seattle by Thursday night. It could be the Jets, Browns, or Steelers moving up, but I rolled with Arizona. Simpson is a high-IQ player who needs a good situation to develop. He's more of a distributor who wins with his mind rather than elite arm talent. He's only got 15 career starts — all in 2025 — with his accuracy and composure notably dipping during the final stretch of the season. Simpson set an Alabama record with a 0.95% interception rate (only 5 INTs on 473 attempts in 2025), but 4 of them came November 22 or later. He tended to struggle when the lights shined brightest — losing to Georgia in the SEC Championship and Indiana in the Rose Bowl, with Bama scoring just 10 points total across those two games. A Bama fan on Reddit put it perfectly: "If the real Ty Simpson is the guy from the first half of the season, then he's possibly a mid-pack starting NFL QB with high IQ and toughness. If the real Ty Simpson is the guy from the second half of the season, then he's probably nothing more than a smart and tough backup." Exactly.
